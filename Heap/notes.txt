2. The insertion algorithm is always:
Put the new element at the end of the heap (to keep the tree complete).
Compare it with its parent.
If the heap property is violated:
In a min-heap, swap while the child is smaller than the parent.
In a max-heap, swap while the child is larger than the parent.
Stop when the heap property is restored.

This is why insertion takes O(log n) time in the worst case—the new element can travel from the bottom of the tree to the root.
